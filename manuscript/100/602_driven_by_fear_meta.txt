## Article strategic - feat. Barbar's scqa

* abstract - The article discusses the role of fear in the entrepreneurial journey, challenging the perception that fear is always negative but focusing on fear as being a motivator element based on value systems. It highlights how fear can act as a catalyst for holding decisions and may impact creativity and growth. By embracing fear and planning ahead, entrepreneurs can navigate challenges and inspire others. Ultimately, the point is to respect fear, as the premise to be more conscious about how value systems are strong in decision making. 

* subject - Fear can move mountains. We should respect fear. 

* situation - We fear things, it's in our genes. But when we fear, in realtime, there is no agenda for reflecting about why we are fearing. Thus we continue to fear, or to sometimes be fearless, in front of what can happen, as plan our future, like in an entrepreneurial journey. Thus, the role of fear, is strong in the individual level and it makes a point, and it impacts group decision.

* complication - We fail to see that fears in the entrepreneurial setting is a strong factor influencing our decisions. When we fear, we fear based on perceptions, but we might translate fears as structured narratives. Thus, there is no space for "I want to talk about my fears in general" and the narrative goes more like "We will be killed by our competition if we do not engage in competing with them." Thus, the fear can be a driver for construction of narratives, for establishing value systems that can govern decisions. Fear, therefore, needs to be respected as it pushes us to be creative and to make decisions or narratives. 

* qa - Can we get rid of fears? Not really and no need, as fear can be good and can regulate. As we do not want to be fearless, we want to simply reflect and respect fear. We can, however, reflect about situations where fear is connected with a perceived or real identify, and with a group identity, or identity that protects a goal. Identitying that, we can be mindful about fear.
